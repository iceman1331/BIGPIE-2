Proposed Implementation Overview

The BIG PIE aims to create an alternative therapy for diabetes. Our pill iBETA is capable of producing insulin in-vivo removing the need for insulin injections.

Our proposed implementation took our project from the lab to the final product for our patients - the Pill iBETA.

The first stage to take our project from proof of concept in the lab to a product is to convert into a well-designed drug in a capsule that easily accessible, ingestible and convenient for the patients. Our survey data showed that over 83% of the people who took our survey preferred tablets or capsules. Hence it was the perfect option for a diabetes drug.

Our bacteria are enclosed in a pH-sensitive pill designed to maximise bacteria release in our target site the duodenum. To improve the safety and efficiency of the bacteria we are using microspheres with mucopenetrative formulations capable of directly penetrating the thick mucous lining and releasing the bacteria in the crypts of the villi.

Find out the details of our drug delivery capsule design.

Our email correspondence with the Central Drug Standards Control Organisation and Endocrinologist from AIIMS Jodhpur, India, helped us understand the ethical and technical concerns involved in our project, as well as the drug safety and regulations. As per the CDSCO, our probiotic would be qualified as a new drug and would need to undergo rigorous clinical trials before being approved as a safe drug in the market.

As part of our implementation, we have created a roadmap that we intend to follow to help our project go from the lab to the market.

Find out the details of the Clinical trials we have designed.

Our pill iBETA is a product being launched into the pharmaceutical market. While our customers are clearly diabetes patients. We have done a thorough analysis of the market dynamics, the competitors, customer needs, requirements, demographics. Understanding the customer expectations, the assistance we can give to the diabetes patients. How we intend to reach and diversify our market. The challenges we will face while entering the market and growing in it.

Our safety policies are also extremely stringent which well ensure the safety not only of the patient but also the environment. We have rigorously tested for any biosecurity issues and tried to eliminate them in the design stages itself.
